Synapse

Privacy Policy · Last updated April 2026

tl;dr

Your browsing history never leaves your browser. All processing happens on your device. We collect nothing, transmit nothing, and have no servers.

What Synapse captures

After you spend 15 or more seconds actively reading a page, Synapse records a small snapshot of that visit to build your personal knowledge base. That snapshot contains:

  • Page title and URL
  • The article or main body text
  • Time spent on the page

Capture only activates on pages with 150 or more words — short pages, dashboards, and navigation pages are automatically skipped. Synapse will never capture activity on login pages, checkout pages, or any domain you have added to your blocklist.

What Synapse never does

  • Makes external API calls of any kind
  • Collects telemetry, analytics, or crash reports
  • Syncs data to any cloud service
  • Requires you to create an account
  • Sells, shares, or monetises your data in any way
  • Allows third-party access to your reading history
  • Embeds tracking pixels or remote resources

Where your data lives

Everything Synapse stores is written to IndexedDB — a local database built into your browser. This storage is sandboxed to the extension and is inaccessible to websites, other extensions, or any external party.

Your data is never transmitted anywhere. It persists on your device even if you uninstall Chrome, and is only removed when you explicitly delete it through Synapse's settings, or clear your browser's local storage data.

The AI model

Synapse uses all-MiniLM-L6-v2 to generate semantic embeddings that power its search and recall features. The model runs entirely inside your browser via ONNX WebAssembly — no GPU servers, no cloud inference, no text ever leaves your machine.

The model file is downloaded once from a CDN and then cached locally. After that first download, Synapse operates with zero network activity.

Your controls

You are in complete control of your data at all times:

  • Export your entire knowledge base as JSON at any time
  • Delete all stored data permanently with a single click
  • Block any domain from being captured via the blocklist
  • Disable capture entirely from the Settings panel

Open source

Synapse is MIT licensed and fully open source. Every line of code — from capture logic to embedding pipeline to the storage layer — is publicly auditable. There are no hidden dependencies, no obfuscated scripts, and no proprietary components.

Read the source code on GitHub: github.com/ManeeshJupalle/Synapse

Questions?

If you have any questions, concerns, or suggestions about this privacy policy or how Synapse handles data, please open an issue on GitHub. We read every report.
